Teacher Matt's Grammar Dad Jokes

"Why did the scarecrow become a motivational speaker?"
"Why did the scarecrow become a motivational speaker?"
"Because he was outstanding in his field!"
Grammar Lesson:
Joke Explanation: This joke is a pun. "Outstanding in his field" means the scarecrow is excellent (outstanding), but it also plays on the literal idea of being physically in a field, where scarecrows are typically placed.
- "the" and "a" are articles, words that introduce nouns.
- "scarecrow" and "field" are nouns, a person, place, or thing, acting as the subject or object.
- "become" is a verb, expressing an action or state.
- "he" and "his" are pronouns, replacing nouns.
- "outstanding" is an adjective, describing the noun "scarecrow".
- "in" is a preposition, showing a relationship between nouns.
Grammar Key: Nouns, Verbs, Adjectives, Adverbs, Articles, Prepositions, Conjunctions, Pronouns
